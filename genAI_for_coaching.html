Generative AI for Enhanced Fitness Coaching

Integrating Generative AI for Enhanced Fitness Coaching: From Exercise Form to Posture and Body Composition Analysis

Since the emergence of ChatGPT, it has shown potential as a smart assistant for fitness. Several applications and specialized versions of ChatGPT have been developed to create personalized exercise programs and provide post-exercise nutrition and recovery advice. However, these versions of ChatGPT often provide overly general suggestions and lack strong visual aids. In this paper, we introduce a specialized ChatGPT as a personalized assistant capable of correcting improper postures and analyzing body compositions. We trained ChatGPT to improve posture during exercises and daily activities, aiming to prevent injuries and optimize training effectiveness.

Additionally, we explored ChatGPT’s potential to understand body compositions and predict physical transformations, providing users with insights into the outcomes of their fitness and nutrition efforts. We focused on investigating the limitations and potential of the current ChatGPT in the sports domain to identify future challenges. Our results indicate that the current ChatGPT model can effectively identify and correct incorrect postures. In terms of body composition, ChatGPT can analyze body fat on a larger scale but is less sensitive to smaller changes in body fat. To further improve ChatGPT's performance, more image datasets on gym exercises, human postures, and body compositions are needed.

Challenges in Applying LLMs for Fitness Coaching

LLMs offer the potential for more accessible, convenient, and cost-effective training guidance. However, our research identifies several challenges in applying LLMs for this purpose:

  • Overstandardized Training Plans: The training plans generated by LLMs tend to be predefined and lack customization to meet individuals’ specific needs and goals.
  • Challenges in Creating Accurate Training Action Images: LLMs struggle to produce images that correctly represent the logical sequence of training actions, limiting their effectiveness in providing clear, actionable guidance.
  • Insufficient Data on Gym Equipment Training and Human Body Metrics: There is a lack of datasets for detailed equipment training techniques and evaluating human body compositions. This lack of data limits the ability of LLMs to offer precise and helpful feedback on body composition and the correct execution of exercises.
  • Ethical and Copyright Concerns with Image Generation: Generating images based on real-life photographs of individuals can raise ethical concerns, including privacy and consent issues. Similarly, creating images derived from virtual characters can lead to copyright infringement problems, posing significant legal challenges.
